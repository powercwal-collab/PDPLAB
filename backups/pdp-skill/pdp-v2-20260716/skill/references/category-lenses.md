# Category Lenses

Use this file when tailoring scoring and recommendations by category.

## Kidswear / Kids Shoes

Parent decision logic:

- Safety first: anti-slip, flexible sole, support, material safety.
- Comfort and growth: foot shape, high instep, wide feet, growth allowance.
- Ease: easy-on/off, washable, durable, school/daycare scenarios.
- Trust: standards, parent reviews, brand kids expertise.

High-impact examples:

- "适合谁/不适合谁" module.
- "脚长 + 0.5cm" growth allowance guidance.
- Anti-slip, bending, heel support tests.
- Child self-wear or parent one-hand assist video.

### Kids Performance Shoes

Use this stricter lens for children's basketball shoes, running shoes, training shoes, and toddler sport shoes where parents need both safety reassurance and sport-performance clarity.

Parent decision questions:

- Is it for the child's age stage and size range: toddler, preschool, grade-school, big kids.
- Is the sport level clear: entry, progression, professional, daily play, school sport, competition.
- Is the use scenario clear: indoor court, outdoor court, running, jumping, daycare, school, mixed daily wear.
- Does it reduce injury anxiety: anti-slip outsole, traction pattern, cushioning, lockdown, heel stability, toe protection, flexible forefoot.
- Does it reduce comfort anxiety: soft tongue, collar comfort, breathable or skin-friendly lining, weight, fit for wide feet or high instep.
- Does it reduce durability anxiety: wear-resistant outsole, reinforced upper, easy cleaning, material resilience.
- Does it help parents choose fast: size range, measurement method, age-stage entry, related toddler/big-kids version, current-viewing versus recommended version.

Visible evidence to check:

- First two screens show age stage, core selling points, sport scenario, and quick choice labels.
- Selling points are mapped to shoe parts, such as midsole cushioning, upper lockdown, outsole traction, tongue softness, toe bumper, heel support.
- Scenario images show children moving in the real sport context, not only static product angles.
- On-foot images show full-body outfit and close-up wearing effect.
- Color options use memorable nicknames or visual swatches, ideally paired with on-foot images.
- Product information includes dimensions, weight, style number, material or structure, reflective details, and color names.
- Size module includes measurement method, multi-age size table, and cross-entry between toddler and big-kids versions.
- Recommendations connect to scenario, outfit, sport level, or same-series comparison rather than generic "you may also like".

## Sports Outdoor

Decision logic:

- Performance trust: terrain, weather, intensity, safety.
- Proof: materials, testing, technology, athlete or expert evidence.
- Scenario: hiking, running, training, commuting, camping.
- Community: clubs, challenges, UGC, expert guides.

Visual direction:

- T1 pages should clearly show terrain, weather, intensity, gear details, fit, and use scenarios.
- T0 pages should create a performance world with motion, outdoor context, weather proof, athlete/expert signals, and memorable product hero scenes.

## Brand Apparel

Decision logic:

- Style identity: silhouette, outfit, trend, lifestyle.
- Fit: size, body type, model measurements, fabric stretch.
- Series story: collection, drop, styling logic.
- Repeat purchase: outfit bundles, color matrix, member hooks.

Visual direction:

- T1 pages should show silhouette, fabric texture, front/back/side fit, model measurements, color, and outfit options.
- T0 pages should use editorial styling, lifestyle mood, movement, series story, and stronger visual rhythm to build desire.

## 3C Electronics

Decision logic:

- Specs hierarchy, not spec dumping.
- Use-case fit, ecosystem compatibility, warranty, comparison.
- Demonstration: video, interaction, scenario workflow.

## Health And Wellness

Decision logic:

- Efficacy, safety, ingredient clarity, routine building.
- Professional credibility and compliance-sensitive wording.
- Before/after claims need careful evidence boundaries.

## Beauty, Bags, Luxury

Decision logic:

- Texture, craft, identity signal, gifting, premium service.
- Scarcity, provenance, craftsmanship, styling scenarios.
- Details and service can carry more conversion weight than discounts.

Visual direction:

- Bags and accessories need capacity, compartment, carrying comfort, material, zipper/hardware, on-body scale, and scenario proof.
- Luxury or premium pages need high-quality light, composition, craft details, provenance, and restrained but memorable editorial layout.
