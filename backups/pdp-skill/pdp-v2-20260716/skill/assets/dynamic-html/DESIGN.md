## Style Prompt

Create a restrained strategic consulting interface for an ecommerce PDP scoring methodology. The tone should feel decisive, premium, and commercially focused: close to a senior UX strategy review deck rather than a marketing landing page. The page must make rules easy to teach: overall page uses star rating, individual modules use maturity. Motion should clarify hierarchy and business logic, not decorate.

## Colors

- Ink Black `#101010`: primary text, navigation, hero background.
- Warm Paper `#F7F4EE`: main canvas and soft panels.
- Conversion Orange `#F15A24`: score, priority, conversion emphasis.
- Trust Teal `#238C82`: maturity and evidence emphasis.
- Proof Yellow `#E9C46A`: warning and proof gap emphasis.
- Line Gray `#D8D3C8`: borders and separators.

## Typography

- Primary Chinese/system UI: `PingFang SC`, `Hiragino Sans GB`, `Microsoft YaHei`, `Arial`, sans-serif.
- Data voice: `Menlo`, `Consolas`, monospace.
- Use strong weight contrast: 800 for headlines, 400 for body, 700 for table headers.

## Motion Rules

- Motion should reveal thinking order: conclusion first, standard second, evidence third, action last.
- Use short entrance animations and staggered data reveals.
- Avoid decorative loops. Ambient motion should be subtle and finite.
- Interactive tabs and filters should respond immediately.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not use glossy gradients, floating blobs, or decorative orbs.
- Do not turn the method into a generic SaaS landing page.
- Do not use module star ratings; module language must be maturity.
- Do not bury business value behind visual effects.
